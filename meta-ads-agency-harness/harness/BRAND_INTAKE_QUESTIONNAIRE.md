# Brand Intake Questionnaire

## Purpose

Use this when a brand owner says they are ready to run ads.

The model should act like an experienced ecommerce marketing agency. It should not jump straight into campaign setup. First, it should collect the minimum information needed to avoid wasting spend.

## Intake Rule

Ask in focused sections. Do not overwhelm the owner with every question at once.

Start with the fast readiness check. Then ask deeper questions only where data is missing.

## Section 1: Fast Readiness Check

Ask these first:

1. What is the Shopify/store URL?
2. What product do you want to advertise first?
3. What is the product price and average order value?
4. What is the gross margin after product cost, payment fees, and shipping?
5. Which country or region do you want to sell in first?
6. Do you already have Meta Pixel and Conversions API installed?
7. How much can you spend per day for the first 7 days?
8. Do you have creative assets ready, such as product photos, UGC videos, testimonials, or founder videos?

## Section 2: Brand And Product Context

- Brand name.
- Category.
- Main products.
- Best-selling product.
- Product price range.
- Average order value.
- Gross margin.
- Repeat purchase rate, if known.
- Inventory constraints.
- Fulfillment time.
- Shipping regions.
- Refund/return rate.
- Any compliance restrictions.

## Section 3: Customer

- Who is the buyer?
- Who is the user, if different from the buyer?
- What problem do they have?
- What are they currently using instead?
- What triggers them to buy?
- What objections stop them?
- What outcome do they want?
- What proof do we have that the product works?

## Section 4: Offer

- Current offer.
- Discount, bundle, gift, or guarantee.
- Delivery promise.
- Return policy.
- Payment options.
- Any urgency or scarcity.
- What makes the offer believable?
- Why should someone buy now?

## Section 5: Store Readiness

- Product page URL.
- Mobile page speed.
- Product images/videos.
- Reviews and testimonials.
- FAQ section.
- Shipping and return clarity.
- Clear CTA above the fold.
- Checkout working.
- Payment methods enabled.
- Abandoned cart flow enabled.

## Section 6: Tracking Readiness

- Meta Pixel installed.
- Conversions API installed.
- Purchase event firing.
- AddToCart event firing.
- InitiateCheckout event firing.
- Domain verified in Meta.
- Aggregated Event Measurement configured, if needed.
- UTMs planned.
- Shopify and Meta attribution expectations aligned.

## Section 7: Past Performance

- Have ads been run before?
- Monthly ad spend.
- Best campaign result.
- Worst campaign result.
- Current CPA.
- Current ROAS.
- Best creative.
- Best offer.
- Email/SMS list size.
- Organic channels working now.

## Section 8: Launch Constraints

- Max daily budget.
- Max monthly budget.
- Minimum acceptable ROAS.
- Maximum acceptable cost per purchase.
- Cash flow constraints.
- Stock constraints.
- Regions to avoid.
- Products to avoid.
- Claims we cannot make.

## Required Minimum Before Strategy

Do not create a launch strategy until these are known:

- Product to advertise.
- Product price.
- Gross margin or target CPA.
- Target region.
- Daily launch budget.
- Store/product page URL.
- Offer.
- Creative asset status.
- Pixel/tracking status.

## Output After Intake

After collecting the required minimum, summarize:

```text
Brand:
Product:
Market:
Offer:
Target customer:
Budget:
Margin/CPA guardrail:
Creative readiness:
Tracking readiness:
Missing risks:
Next step:
```

## Red Flags

Pause and warn the owner if:

- No working product page.
- No purchase tracking.
- Unknown margin and no target CPA.
- Budget too low to get meaningful learning.
- Product has compliance-sensitive claims.
- Store has obvious trust issues.
- Owner expects guaranteed profit from day one.
