# Offer Strategy Workflow

## Purpose

Use this before creating the final ad strategy.

This workflow combines:

- `offer-extraction`
- local `100m-offers`
- competitor analysis
- Shopify/store readiness

The goal is to produce an offer strong enough to deserve paid traffic.

## Inputs

Required:

- Brand intake summary.
- Product selected for ads.
- Price.
- Margin or target CPA.
- Target customer.
- Target region.
- Current offer.
- Competitor brief.
- Store readiness notes.

Optional:

- Reviews and testimonials.
- Past ad results.
- Customer objections.
- Founder story.
- Product demos.
- Bundle options.

## Step 1: Clarify Avatar

Answer:

- Who exactly is this for?
- What urgent problem do they have?
- What do they already believe?
- What have they tried before?
- What do they fear will happen if they choose wrong?

Output:

```text
Specific avatar:
Pain:
Dream outcome:
Nightmare avoided:
Past failed attempts:
```

## Step 2: Apply The Value Equation

Increase:

- Dream outcome.
- Perceived likelihood of achievement.

Decrease:

- Time delay.
- Effort and sacrifice.

Output:

```text
Dream outcome:
Proof or mechanism that increases belief:
Fastest meaningful result:
Effort removed:
```

## Step 3: Build The Value Stack

List the obstacles that stop the buyer from getting the result.

Create one component or bonus for each obstacle.

```text
Obstacle:
Component:
What it does:
Standalone value:
```

Rules:

- Use only real components or clearly label proposed components.
- Do not invent fake bonuses as if they already exist.
- Bonuses should solve objections, not pad the offer.

## Step 4: Create Risk Reversal

Pick the strongest honest guarantee the business can support.

Options:

- Simple return/refund policy.
- Satisfaction guarantee.
- Performance guarantee.
- Service guarantee.
- No-risk trial.
- Transparent anti-guarantee, only if the brand intentionally uses one.

Output:

```text
Guarantee:
Why it is believable:
Operational risk:
```

## Step 5: Add Real Urgency Or Scarcity

Use only real reasons:

- Limited inventory.
- Launch window.
- Seasonal timing.
- Bonus expiry.
- Cohort closing.
- Price increase.
- Shipping deadline.

Do not use fake countdowns or fake scarcity.

## Step 6: Name The Offer

Use:

```text
[Timeframe] + [Dream outcome] + [Objection killer]
```

Create three names:

1.
2.
3.

## Step 7: Final Offer Output

```text
TARGET AVATAR:
DREAM OUTCOME:
NIGHTMARE AVOIDED:
CORE MECHANISM:
DOMINANT OFFER:
VALUE STACK:
GUARANTEE:
URGENCY/SCARCITY:
OFFER NAME OPTIONS:
ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH:
RISKS/ASSUMPTIONS:
```

## Pass/Fail Check

The offer is not ready if:

- The avatar is vague.
- The dream outcome is generic.
- There is no proof.
- There is no risk reversal.
- The value stack is just a list of features.
- The urgency is fake.
- The price is impossible to justify against margin or value.
