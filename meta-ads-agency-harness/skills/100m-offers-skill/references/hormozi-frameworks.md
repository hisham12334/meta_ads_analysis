# Hormozi Deep Reference: Extended Frameworks

## The Misery Index — Finding the Best Market

Hormozi says the best market to enter has 3 qualities:

1. **Massive pain** — People are actively suffering and desperate for a solution
2. **Purchasing power** — They have money (or access to money) to solve the problem
3. **Easy to target** — You can find and reach them (Facebook group, subreddit, conference, etc.)

The three evergreen markets that always have pain + money:
- **Health** (lose weight, cure pain, look better, live longer)
- **Wealth** (make money, career, business growth, investment)
- **Relationships** (dating, marriage, social skills, confidence)

Sub-niches within these always work best. "Help divorced men over 40 get back into dating" beats "dating coach."

## The Value Equation — Extended Examples

### Example: Online Fitness Coach

**Before applying the equation:**
- "12-week online fitness program — $297"

**After applying all 4 levers:**
- Dream Outcome ↑: "Lose 20 lbs and feel confident in your body again"
- Likelihood ↑: "97% of our clients hit their goal — here's why our method works when everything else failed"
- Time ↓: "See results in your first 2 weeks — guaranteed"
- Effort ↓: "No gym required. 20-minute home workouts. Meals you actually enjoy."

**Resulting offer name:** "The 20-lb Drop Method: Lose Your First 10 Pounds in 14 Days — From Home, No Gym, No Starving — or Your Money Back"

## The MAGIC Naming Formula

When naming bonuses and components, use:

- **M**ake a claim (the result)
- **A**ttribute a timeframe
- **G**ive it a unique name
- **I**dentify who it's for (optional)
- **C**ontrast with what they'd normally have to do

**Example:**
- Claim: Get your first 3 clients
- Timeframe: In 30 days
- Unique name: "The Cold Outreach Accelerator"
- Who: For new coaches with no audience
- Contrast: Without spending on ads or posting daily

**Result:** "The Cold Outreach Accelerator: Get Your First 3 Clients in 30 Days — No Audience, No Ads Required"

## Bonus Sequencing Strategy

Hormozi teaches that bonuses should be sequenced to address objections in order of importance:

1. **Bonus 1** — Solves the most common objection ("I don't have time")
2. **Bonus 2** — Solves the second most common objection ("I'm not tech-savvy")
3. **Bonus 3** — Speeds up the path to result (fast-start win)
4. **Bonus 4** — Addresses the emotional fear ("What if I fail again?")
5. **Bonus 5** — Social proof accelerator or community element

## Guarantee Deep Dive

### The Math of Bold Guarantees

Hormozi's insight: If your product actually works, refund rates on bold guarantees are **lower** than on weak guarantees — because:
1. Bold guarantees attract more confident, motivated buyers
2. Buyers who believe in the guarantee trust you, and trust = compliance = results
3. Refunds usually come from buyers who were never sure — weak guarantees let unsure people in at volume

### Writing the Performance Guarantee

Template:
> "If you [complete the actions] and don't [achieve the result] within [timeframe], I will [what you'll do] — no questions asked. I can make this promise because [reason your method works that they need to know]."

Example:
> "If you complete all 6 modules, post your offer in our community for feedback, and reach out to 30 prospects in 30 days — and still don't land a single client — I'll personally work with you 1-on-1 until you do, for free. I can make this promise because every client who has done those three things has gotten results. The only way you lose is if you don't show up."

## Scarcity & Urgency — Real Examples

### Real Scarcity (ethical):
- Coaching/consulting has natural capacity limits → use them honestly
- "I run 2 live cohorts per year" → cohort closes are real deadlines
- "I have 3 spots in my 1:1 program" → genuinely limited

### Real Urgency (ethical):
- Bonus with a real expiry (you'll remove it after the launch)
- Price increase tied to a launch (publicly announced in advance)
- Seasonal relevance ("This is the best time to start before the holiday rush")

### What NOT to do:
- Fake countdown timers that reset on page reload
- "Only 3 left!" on a digital product with infinite copies
- Manufactured scarcity the prospect can easily see through

## One-Liner Offer Test

Before finalizing the offer, test it with this one-liner framework:

> "I help [specific avatar] achieve [dream outcome] in [timeframe] without [biggest objection/fear]."

If you can't say it clearly in one sentence, the offer is not clear enough yet.

**Examples:**
- "I help new online coaches land their first 3 paying clients in 30 days without a big following or paid ads."
- "I help e-commerce store owners scale to $50K/month in 90 days without hiring a team or running complicated funnels."
- "I help burned-out 9-to-5ers replace their salary with freelance income in 6 months — no portfolio, no experience required."

## Offer Positioning by Product Type

### Course / Self-Study Program
- Lower price point ($97–$997)
- Must deliver a VERY specific, fast result
- Bonuses are critical to differentiate
- Community element increases retention and perceived value
- Guarantee must be outcome-based (or you'll get refund abuse)

### Group Coaching Program
- Mid-to-high ticket ($1,000–$5,000)
- Cohort model creates urgency naturally
- Live Q&A calls are your highest-value component — price accordingly
- Stack: Program + Calls + Community + Templates + 1:1 kickoff call

### 1:1 Coaching / Consulting
- High ticket ($3,000–$25,000+)
- Sold via application + call, not checkout page
- Value stack still matters for the sales call
- Guarantee: "Work with you until you get the result" (performance-based)
- Scarcity is naturally real — use your actual availability

### Done-For-You Service
- Premium pricing ($2,500–$50,000+)
- Effort lever is maxed (they do nothing — highest value)
- Delivery speed and proof are the key trust builders
- Guarantee: Deliverables-based ("We will deliver X by Y date or you pay nothing")
